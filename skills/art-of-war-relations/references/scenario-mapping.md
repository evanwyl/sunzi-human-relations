# Scenario mapping

Use this file to avoid answers that are technically correct but too generic.

## Workplace
Focus on:
- role clarity
- credit and blame
- resource competition
- leader expectations
- alliances, process, and timing

Common hidden dynamics:
- status protection
- narrative control
- asymmetric risk
- informal power vs formal title

Common subtypes:
- leader dumps risk downward
- peer steals credit quietly
- soft exclusion from key information
- public praise, private sacrifice
- using “team spirit” to erase contribution boundaries

## Friendship
Focus on:
- reciprocity
- emotional labor imbalance
- expectation mismatch
- circle politics
- boundary drift

Common hidden dynamics:
- convenience without commitment
- resentment hidden under politeness
- uneven investment

Common subtypes:
- one-sided emotional dumping
- intermittent warmth to keep access
- disguised disrespect as joking
- being useful but not valued

## Romance / couple
Focus on:
- trust
- repair or rupture
- recurring conflict pattern
- emotional timing
- whether the fight is about the stated topic or unmet needs underneath

Common hidden dynamics:
- reassurance demand vs autonomy demand
- power through distance or silence
- unresolved prior hurt

Common subtypes:
- one side always repairs
- repeated apology without structural change
- affection withdrawal as control
- conflict about small issues covering bigger unmet needs

## Ambiguous relationship
Focus on:
- initiative and reciprocity
- uncertainty tolerance
- testing vs withdrawal
- whether ambiguity benefits one side more than the other

Common hidden dynamics:
- desire mismatch
- attention without commitment
- timing mismatch
- asymmetry in willingness to define the relationship

Common subtypes:
- hot-cold attention loop
- contact without commitment
- emotional closeness without future movement
- reappearing when the user is about to leave

## Use rule

Do not stop at the broad scene label. Try to identify the subtype.
A strong answer usually names both:
- the big scene
- the actual subtype of the situation
